Top Beaches in the world

Playa del Amor, Marietas Islands, Mexico:

A swim through an opening nearly invisible from the sea reveals what locals call the “hidden beach,” encircled by an impressive rock ring forming a natural oculus for the sun and sky. Only six visitors at a time can visit “Love Beach”.

Beach of the Cathedrals, Spain:

The Beach of the Cathedrals is a stunning beach located in the Ribadeo municipality in Spain. It is known for its towering rock formations that resemble the arches of a cathedral.

The Baths, British Virgin Islands:

The Baths is a beach area on the island of Virgin Gorda among the British Virgin Islands in the Caribbean. The Baths are characterized by naturally formed granite boulders, some of which stand over 30 feet tall.

Boulders Beach, South Africa:

Boulders Beach is a sheltered beach made up of inlets between granite boulders, from which the name originated. It is located in the Cape Peninsula, near Simon’s Town towards Cape Point, near Cape Town in the Western Cape province of South Africa.
